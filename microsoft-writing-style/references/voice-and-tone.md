# Voice and tone

Source: [Microsoft brand voice](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/brand-voice-above-all-simple-human)

## Three voice principles

The Microsoft voice hinges on crisp simplicity. Bigger ideas and fewer words. Less head, more heart.

1. **Warm and relaxed** — Natural, less formal, grounded in everyday conversation. Occasionally fun (we know when to celebrate).
2. **Crisp and clear** — To the point. Write for scanning first, reading second. Simple above all.
3. **Ready to lend a hand** — Show customers we're on their side. Anticipate needs, offer information at the right time.

## Voice vs. tone

- **Voice** is constant — it's our personality regardless of audience or topic.
- **Tone** adapts to context: serious for errors, empathetic for failures, lighthearted for celebrations.

## Style tips

- **Get to the point fast.** Start with the key takeaway. Put the most important thing in the most noticeable spot. Make choices and next steps obvious. Don't get in the way.
- **Talk like a person.** Choose optimistic, conversational language. Use short everyday words, contractions, and sentence-style capitalization. Shun jargon and acronyms.
- **Simpler is better.** Break it up. Step it out. Layer. Short sentences and fragments are easier to scan and read. Prune every excess word.

## Writing like you speak

- Read your text aloud. If it sounds stilted, rewrite it.
- Use contractions: *it's, you'll, you're, we're, let's, don't, isn't.*
- Avoid overly formal constructions (*utilize* → *use*, *in order to* → *to*, *at this point in time* → *now*).
- Use second person (*you*) to address the reader directly.
- Use first person plural (*we*) when speaking as the product or organization.

## Adapting tone by scenario

| Scenario | Tone | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| Error message | Empathetic, helpful | "Something went wrong. Try again, or contact support if it keeps happening." |
| Success confirmation | Warm, celebratory | "You're all set! Your changes are saved." |
| Instruction | Clear, direct | "Select the file, then choose Share." |
| Warning | Serious, respectful | "This action can't be undone. Make sure you're ready." |
| Marketing | Enthusiastic, concise | "Do more with less. Try it free." |

## Common anti-patterns to fix

| Anti-pattern | Problem | Better |
|---|---|---|
| "It should be noted that..." | Filler phrase | Delete and start with the real content |
| "In order to configure..." | Wordy | "To configure..." |
| "The user is able to..." | Passive, wordy | "You can..." or start with verb |
| "Please be advised that..." | Overly formal | State the information directly |
| "It is recommended that..." | Impersonal passive | "We recommend..." or use imperative |
| "Utilize" | Jargon | "Use" |
| "Leverage" (as verb) | Jargon | "Use" or "take advantage of" |
| "Functionality" | Bloated | "Features" or be specific |
